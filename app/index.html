        log A logogram is a sign that can stand for a word by itself, mostly without phonograms. Some logograms however can stand for several different, etymologically unrelated words.

        det A determinative is a sign that mostly occurs following phonograms (and sometimes logograms) and that serves to narrow down or emphasize the meaning.

        log or det A sign is included in this class if it can be used as logogram, but may alternatively be accompanied (usually preceded) by phonograms or other logograms.

        phon A phonogram is a sign that stands for a sequence of consonants, used in writings of words irrespective of their meaning.

        phon det A phonetic determinative functions much as a phonogram, and has an interpretation as a sequence of consonants, as indicated in the table. Unlike phonograms however, a phonetic determinative cannot stand on its own and must follow one or more phonograms together covering the same consonants. Phonetic determinatives usually stand at the end of a word, next to any (semantic) determinatives.

        phon or phon det This class includes signs that are used both as phonetic determinatives and as ordinary phonograms.

        typ The class of typographical symbols includes all signs that do not fall into any of the above categories.
